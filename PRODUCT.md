# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors are academic research audiences: faculty, collaborators, prospective graduate students, conference attendees, and people looking for Haidan Liu / Afra's current research profile. They are usually scanning for research area, advisor context, publications, contact details, and credibility.

## Product Purpose

This is a personal academic website for research purposes. It should quickly communicate who Afra is, what she studies, where she is based, and which publications or research outputs matter most. Success means visitors can understand the research focus, verify academic context, and find the right external links without friction.

## Brand Personality

Clean, minimalist, academic. The tone should feel clear, thoughtful, approachable, and credible without becoming corporate or overly formal.

## Anti-references

Avoid anything too corporate, too playful, too template-like, too flashy, or too complicated. Avoid generic AI landing-page patterns, heavy animation, decorative gradients, dense card grids, and interface language that makes the site feel like a product UI.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with academic clarity: research identity and publications should be easy to scan.
2. Preserve restraint: use whitespace, typography, and simple hierarchy instead of decoration.
3. Keep the voice human: approachable enough for prospective students, credible enough for research peers.
4. Prefer content over effects: motion, visual assets, and layout changes must support comprehension.
5. Stay personal, not corporate: the site should feel like an individual researcher, not an institutional landing page.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and links. Keep motion minimal and non-essential. Preserve readable font sizes, short line lengths, visible focus states, and clear link affordances for visitors scanning quickly on desktop or mobile.
